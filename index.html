About.

Hello again. My name is James Jr. Njong, and I am a versatile problem solver with a diverse set of skills and cross-functional knowledge that affords me the latitude to tackle problems in software development, financial and data analysis as well as advance mathematics. I have been privileged to work within education management over the last two years, expanding my knowledge of project management and also mastering the art of communicating complex ideas, while building partnerships that help advance our goals as an organization.
At present, I am keen on new challenges that will leverage some or all of my diverse problem solving skills, and I continue to think that a tech-savvy problem solver with management experience and a natural ability to communicate effectively, can seamlessly fit-in and thrive at all levels of any value chain.

Outlook

I am extremely excited about work that puts me at the intersection of business and product development. I find that these kinds of roles allow me to marry my ability to communicate and to solve both technical and non-technical problems using the full spectrum of the skills I have developed. And in this way, my employer gets the full benefits of what (s)he pays for.

Throwback

Having had over a decade of graduate and post graduate level training as a mathematician, I have developed a solid procedural problem solving ability that all but guarantees success in tackling technical problems. And because these years have involved research, I tend to be great at self directing, and thus need minimal supervision on the job.

Motivation

By default, I am not set up as an entrepreneur, and thus my energy entirely comes from doing meaningful work that helps advance an agenda that is not centered around me. I consequently am comfortable within the chaos of a startup environment, as well as the calm of established organizations, both not lacking in ambition, nor uncertain about their vision.
